# What is C2E2?

C2E2 stands for Chicago Comic & Entertainment Expo. Comics, cosplay, gaming, authors, and anime abound in the only pop culture convention in the heart of Downtown Chicago! (This was taken straight from their website.)

# Friday (Day 1)
## Events
For Friday, I had Storytelling in Games with Dechart Games with Bryan Dechart and Amelia Rose Blaire Dechart. I also wanted to attend the Project Hail Mary and the Science Behind the Blockbuster Sci-Fi.

### Bryan and Amelia (Day 1 Panel)
The storytelling panel was really cool. It was in a medium-sized room, and there weren't that many people, so it was a more intimate experience. Both Bryan and Amelia were awesome; they were very engaged, and there was a Q&A at the end so fans could come up and ask their questions. I sometimes think about potentially trying to get into voice acting, and Bryan emphasized getting to know Indie gamers/developers because, as they are making games, they might need a voice actor for their game. 

So I kept that in mind throughout the weekend because voice acting has grown of interest to me over the past year. (I have started to watch more anime)

### Project Hail Mary and the Science Behind Blockbuster Sci-Fi
This panel was pretty awesome. The room ended up getting full with many people, even standing in the back of the room. The panel included Daniel Holz, Gillian King-Cargile, Gregory Grant, Dion Antonopoulos, and Leslie Rogers. It was fascinating to hear the science behind how realistic something like the book can come true and how accurate the science behind it was. They were all so intersted and their answers had depth to the moderator. And that energy continued with a Q&A at the end, and there was so much humor, especially from Gregory. It was so interesting that many continued the conversation outside in the hall after the panel. 

### Brett Dalton (Selfie)
This was not planned, but I did see his table was empty and he was there, so I decided to go up to him and talk. I was really into Agents of Shield the first couple of seasons, where he was one of the main characters of the show. Something that I learned recently was that he was Freyr in God of War: Ragnarok, which has one of my favorite scenes of all time. Kratos tells Atreus that he was wrong and that he should open his heart. That scene still gives me goosebumps til this day.

So I asked him about his personal experience on set. Said it was a wonderful experience and hopes he can do something similar again in the future. It is less physically demanding because of how much time it takes to record compared to being on television. Make sure the cameras are in the correct angles, lighting, makeup, hair, costume, etc. In this one, it was motion capture with 360-degree cameras. The magic happens with the ones creating and designing the environment. It was cool to hear him talk. 

### Bryan & Amelia (Day 1 Autograph)
I had bought a Detroit Become Human Jacket on Thursday, and so I wanted them to sign it. When I first walked up, I was very nervous. I asked them what their favorite project was to work on and why. Was it because you had more control over your character, or was it because you believed in the vision of the director? # Tips that I learned
## Photo Ops
Photos Ops are kinda underwhelming to me. I feel that the price of the picture doesn't live up to the value. A lot of the people who offer the opportunities for a selfie at the table at a lower price, so I think that is more appealing. You don't really get any interaction with the celebrity, as it all goes so fast once you're in there. Sometimes you can take a long time waiting in line, and that takes away your time from doing other things. 

## Autographs
I definitely think this is the way to go if you want a conversation with whoever you want to interact with. Every autograph I got came with a conversation. I got to ask the celebrities questions and just converse with them. If there is no line behind you, then you can just stay there as long as you want and keep talking to them. However, if there is a line behind, then try to be respectful because people also want to talk and get to interact with the person at the table. 

## Parking/Transportation
I would highly recommend taking public transportation to the venue because parking can cost you quite a bit. On top of that, especially on Saturday, it can be heavily congested. It can take a while for you to get there.

I ended up going alone to this convention, which I was nervous about, but I had read online about how friendly everyone was. Everywhere I went, people were so helpful and nice. You can simply strike up a conversation while waiting in line or sitting in a panel. How is your day doing? Or How has the convention been? Each person goes for a different version and goes to different events so you can just keep going from there!

## Art
What is Earth without Art? Just a rock! People are so creative and artistic that everywhere you look, someone has crafted something awesome. 

## Conclusion
I absolutely loved my time at C2E2 because there are so many different things you can do! There is something for everyone in the family, and the panels were great, fun, and interactive. If you have never gone to any sort of comic convention. I would highly recommend that you go, and hope that you can experience the same joy I had at C2E2.
